The Concept of Compound Growth

Compound growth, often referred to as "the snowball effect," explains how investments grow over time, as gains are reinvested to generate additional growth. The Standard & Poor's 500 (S&P 500) index, a benchmark for the U.S. stock market, showcases how consistent and long-term investing can lead to remarkable wealth accumulation over decades.
